# LOCATION EXPLANATIONS

This document provides explainable reasoning for all suitability scores generated for the Namami Gange Demonstration Intelligence Layer.

No score is generated as a black-box value. Every location includes positive factors, negative factors, operational reasoning, and confidence assessment.

---

# Varanasi

## Score

92 / 100

## Suitability Level

High

## Why Score Is High

Varanasi is one of the strongest inland waterway locations along National Waterway-1 due to the presence of an operational multimodal terminal, strong rail and airport connectivity, and a mature logistics ecosystem.

The city also benefits from significant tourism demand and stable navigation conditions.

## Most Important Positive Factors

* Operational Ramnagar Multimodal Terminal
* Airport connectivity available
* Major railway junction
* National highway connectivity
* Proposed Multi Modal Logistics Park (MMLP)
* Strong tourism demand
* High navigability depth (~3.0 m)
* Stable river flow conditions
* Pollution Class B water quality

## Most Important Negative Factors

* Seasonal water level fluctuations
* Moderate environmental sensitivity during peak tourism periods

## Key Opportunities

* Cargo movement
* Passenger ferry operations
* Tourism transport services
* Logistics hub development
* Intermodal freight movement

## Key Constraints

* Seasonal river level variation

## Confidence Score

0.92

## Explanation Summary

Varanasi achieves a high score because infrastructure, navigation conditions, logistics readiness, and economic activity are all favorable. Existing multimodal infrastructure significantly increases operational viability.

# Scoring Methodology

Suitability scores are generated using the following factors:

| Component                  | Weight |
| -------------------------- | ------ |
| Infrastructure Readiness   | 30%    |
| Connectivity               | 25%    |
| Navigation Suitability     | 25%    |
| Demand & Economic Activity | 20%    |

Final suitability levels:

* High: 85–100
* Medium: 70–84
* Low: Below 70

The explanation layer ensures every score can be traced back to observable infrastructure, environmental, and operational factors.
