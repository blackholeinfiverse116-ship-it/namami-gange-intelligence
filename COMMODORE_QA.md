# COMMODORE_QA

## 1. Why is Varanasi scored higher than Kanpur?

Varanasi has better inland waterway infrastructure, stronger tourism activity and better river transport readiness.

The city has an operational multimodal terminal at Ramnagar and strong connectivity through road, rail and air networks.

Kanpur has good industrial demand, but water quality concerns and navigation limitations reduce its overall suitability score.

---

## 2. Which datasets are being used?

The system currently uses five datasets:

* Urban Centers Dataset
* Logistics Parks Dataset
* IWAI Terminals Dataset
* CPCB Water Quality Dataset
* CWC River Monitoring Dataset

Together these datasets help us understand infrastructure, logistics, navigation conditions, environmental quality and economic demand.

---

## 3. How do you know the water quality?

Water quality information comes from CPCB monitoring stations located along the Ganga.

For example:

* Varanasi → Rajghat Station
* Patna → Gandhi Ghat Station
* Prayagraj → Sangam Station
* Kanpur → Jajmau Station

These stations provide the water quality information used in the assessment.

---

## 4. Why should I trust this score?

Every score can be explained.

The system shows:

* Which datasets were used
* Which records were used
* Which factors improved the score
* Which factors reduced the score

Nothing is hidden or generated by a black-box model.

---

## 5. What happens if the dataset is wrong?

If a dataset contains incorrect information, the score may change.

However, because every score is linked to specific datasets and records, it is easy to identify the source of the issue and update the result after correcting the data.

The system is designed to be transparent and traceable.

---

## 6. What factors influence the score the most?

Four main factors affect the final score:

### Infrastructure

Availability of terminals, ports and logistics facilities.

### Connectivity

Road, rail and airport access.

### Navigation

River conditions and suitability for inland water transport.

### Demand

Population, tourism and economic activity.

These factors are combined to produce the final suitability score.

---

## 7. What is the difference between suitability and opportunity?

### Suitability

Shows how ready a location is today.

Example:
A city with good infrastructure and navigation conditions has high suitability.

### Opportunity

Shows future growth potential.

Example:
A city may have moderate suitability today but strong potential for logistics, tourism or cargo expansion in the future.

---

## 8. Can this be expanded to other river locations?

Yes.

The same approach can be applied to additional cities, terminals and river locations.

New locations can be added simply by connecting the relevant datasets and generating the same intelligence outputs.

---

## 9. Is this system live?

No.

For the current showcase, the system uses prepared datasets.

The outputs are generated from those datasets and are intended for demonstration purposes.

---

## 10. Can real-time data be added later?

Yes.

Future versions can connect to:

* River sensors
* Water quality sensors
* Vessel tracking systems
* Weather feeds
* Flood monitoring systems

The dashboard experience remains the same, but the intelligence becomes live and continuously updated.
