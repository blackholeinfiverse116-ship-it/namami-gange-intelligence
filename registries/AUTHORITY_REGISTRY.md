# AUTHORITY REGISTRY

## Purpose

The Authority Registry defines who owns, governs, validates, approves and operates different parts of the Marine Intelligence System.

Every dataset, entity, recommendation, decision, validation result and operational action must have a clearly defined authority.

No intelligence should exist without ownership.

No recommendation should exist without accountability.

No decision should exist without approval authority.

The Authority Registry ensures governance, traceability, responsibility and operational control across the entire Marine Intelligence ecosystem.

---

# Why Authority Matters

Marine Intelligence is not only about data.

It is also about responsibility.

Example:

A flood warning may be generated by telemetry.

But who validates it?

Who approves restrictions?

Who authorizes operational decisions?

Who owns the data?

The Authority Registry answers these questions.

# Authority Conflict Resolution

If two authorities disagree:

1. Validation review initiated.
2. Evidence reviewed.
3. Governance authority consulted.
4. Final decision recorded.
5. Provenance trail updated.

No authority conflict may remain unresolved.

---

# Governance Rules

1. Every entity must have an owner.
2. Every recommendation must have an authority.
3. Every decision must be approved.
4. Every validation must identify a validator.
5. Every authority action must be traceable.
6. Every approval must be auditable.
7. Every authority must have defined scope.

---

# Expected Outcome

The Authority Registry ensures that every piece of intelligence, every recommendation, every validation and every operational decision has a clearly defined owner and responsible authority.

This prevents ambiguity, strengthens governance and ensures that the Marine Intelligence System remains explainable, auditable, deterministic and operationally accountable.
