# CARGO CORRIDOR INTELLIGENCE

## Purpose

The Cargo Corridor Intelligence Model helps identify, assess and optimize cargo movement across inland waterways and supporting logistics networks.

The objective is to transform raw infrastructure, terminal, logistics and demand data into actionable cargo movement intelligence.

The model should answer:

* Where is cargo coming from?
* Where is cargo going? 
* Which corridor should be used?
* What bottlenecks exist?
* Which terminals should be used?
* What risks may affect cargo movement?
* What opportunities exist for cargo expansion?

The system is designed to support operational planning, logistics optimization and future decision-support systems.

# 1. Cargo Origins

## Purpose

Identify where cargo is generated.

Cargo origins represent locations where goods enter the transportation network.

## Examples

* Industrial clusters
* Manufacturing centers
* Agricultural regions
* Warehousing zones
* Logistics parks

## Key Factors

* Production volume
* Economic activity
* Industrial presence
* Population demand

## Example

Location:

Kanpur

Cargo Sources:

* Industrial goods
* Manufacturing products
* Consumer goods

Assessment:

Strong Cargo Origin

# 3. Cargo Corridors

## Purpose

Identify the most efficient routes for cargo movement.

A cargo corridor represents the complete movement path between origin and destination.

## Corridor Types

### Inland Waterway Corridor

Uses river transportation.

Benefits:

* Lower transportation cost
* Reduced road congestion
* Lower emissions

---

### Multimodal Corridor

Uses a combination of:

* Waterways
* Railways
* Roads

Benefits:

* Improved flexibility
* Faster cargo movement
* Better connectivity

---

### Export Corridor

Connects inland locations to ports and trade gateways.

Benefits:

* International market access
* Increased trade opportunities

---

## Example Corridor

Kanpur
→ Prayagraj
→ Varanasi
→ Patna
→ Kolkata

Assessment:

High Strategic Importance

# 4. Hub-Spoke Network Intelligence

## Purpose

Identify major cargo hubs and supporting feeder locations.

### Hub

A major cargo aggregation and distribution center.

Examples:

* Varanasi
* Patna
* Kolkata

---

### Spoke

A smaller location connected to a hub.

Examples:

* Regional cargo collection points
* Local industrial clusters

---

* Better resource utilization
* Reduced transportation costs

---

Strong Hub-Spoke Potential

* Operational status
* Congestion indicators

* Opportunity for additional cargo

Assessment:

---

## High Utilization

* Potential congestion

Operational Risk

---

## Example

---

# 6. Capacity Constraint Intelligence

## Purpose

Identify infrastructure or operational limitations that may affect cargo movement.

## Common Constraints

### Terminal Capacity Constraints

Insufficient handling capacity.

Impact:

Cargo delays

---

### Connectivity Constraints

Poor road, rail or waterway integration.

Impact:

Reduced logistics efficiency

---

### Navigation Constraints

Limited water depth or route restrictions.

Impact:

Reduced vessel movement

---

### Seasonal Constraints

Monsoon and water level variations.

Impact:

Operational uncertainty

# Recommendation Framework

Every corridor assessment should provide:

* Corridor Name
* Cargo Flow Assessment
* Opportunities
* Risks
* Constraints
* Confidence Score
* Recommended Actions

Example:

Corridor:

Varanasi → Patna → Kolkata

Assessment:

High Potential

Confidence:

0.91

Key Opportunity:

Cargo Expansion

Key Constraint:

Seasonal Navigation Variability

Recommended Action:

Increase corridor utilization while monitoring seasonal conditions.

---

# Explainability Requirements

Every cargo intelligence output must answer:

1. Where is cargo coming from?
2. Where is cargo going?
3. Why was this corridor selected?
4. What opportunities exist?
5. What risks exist?
6. What constraints exist?
7. Why is the confidence score high or low?

No cargo recommendation should be generated without supporting evidence.
