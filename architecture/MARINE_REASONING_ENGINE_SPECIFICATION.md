# 1. Purpose

The Marine Reasoning Engine is the cognitive core of NICAI.

Its responsibility is not to store data.

Its responsibility is to transform observations into explainable operational decisions.

The reasoning engine behaves like an experienced Commodore.

It does not simply react to events.

It understands:

* Context
* History
* Constraints
* Risks
* Opportunities
* Operational consequences

Every recommendation generated by NICAI must pass through the Marine Reasoning Engine.

# 3. Objectives

The reasoning engine must answer:

* What happened?
* Why did it happen?
* What caused it?
* What is the current situation?
* What risks exist?
* What opportunities exist?
* What actions are available?
* Which action is best?
* Why is it best?
* What evidence supports it?

# 4. Intelligence Layers

The Marine Reasoning Engine consists of twelve intelligence layers.

---

## Layer 1 — Observation Intelligence

### Purpose

Convert raw data into observations.

### Inputs

* Sensors
* Telemetry
* Reports
* MasterDB Records

### Outputs

Structured Observations

Example:

River Level = 2.8m

Terminal Utilization = 87%

Water Velocity = 1.4 m/s

### Failure Modes

* Missing data
* Sensor failure
* Duplicate observations

---

## Layer 2 — Signal Intelligence

### Purpose

Detect meaningful changes.

### Inputs

Observations

### Outputs

Signals

Examples:

* Water level dropping
* Terminal congestion increasing
* Cargo demand rising

### Failure Modes

* Noise
* Incorrect thresholds

---

## Layer 3 — Pattern Intelligence

### Purpose

Identify recurring behavior.

### Inputs

Signals

### Outputs

Patterns

Examples:

* Seasonal water decline
* Recurring congestion
* Repeated navigation restrictions

### Failure Modes

* Insufficient history
* False pattern detection

---

## Layer 4 — Context Intelligence

### Purpose

Understand operational meaning.

### Inputs

Patterns
Signals
Geography

### Outputs

Operational Context

Example:

Water level decline during dry season is expected.

Water level decline during cargo peak season is critical.

---

## Layer 5 — Historical Intelligence

### Purpose

Retrieve similar past situations.

### Inputs

Current Context

### Outputs

Historical References

Example:

Similar event occurred:

* July 2021
* August 2022
* June 2024

---

## Layer 6 — Predictive Intelligence

### Purpose

Estimate future state.

### Inputs

Current State
Patterns
History

### Outputs

Predictions

Examples:

* Navigation depth may decrease.
* Flood risk may increase.
* Terminal congestion may rise.

### Failure Modes

* Missing historical records
* Incorrect trend assumptions

---

## Layer 7 — Constraint Intelligence

### Purpose

Identify operational limitations.

### Inputs

Predictions
Current State

### Outputs

Constraints

Examples:

* Low navigability
* Restricted draft
* Terminal capacity limits

---

## Layer 8 — Opportunity Intelligence

### Purpose

Identify positive operational possibilities.

### Inputs

Infrastructure
Demand
Connectivity

### Outputs

Opportunities

Examples:

* Cargo expansion
* Ferry services
* Alternate terminal utilization

---

## Layer 9 — Risk Intelligence

### Purpose

Assess operational risk.

### Inputs

Constraints
Predictions

### Outputs

Risk Objects

Examples:

* Flood Risk
* Navigation Risk
* Environmental Risk
* Cargo Delay Risk

---

## Layer 10 — Decision Intelligence

### Purpose

Generate candidate actions.

### Inputs

Risks
Constraints
Opportunities

### Outputs

Decision Options

Examples:

* Continue operations
* Restrict operations
* Divert cargo
* Suspend navigation

---

## Layer 11 — Validation Intelligence

### Purpose

Verify recommendations.

### Inputs

Decision Options

### Outputs

Validated Recommendations

Validation Sources:

* SVACS
* Evidence Memory
* Historical Memory

---

## Layer 12 — Governance Intelligence

### Purpose

Ensure policy compliance.

### Inputs

Validated Recommendation

### Outputs

Approved Decision

Checks:

* Regulatory compliance
* Authority boundaries
* Safety policies
* Environmental restrictions

# 7. Competing Decision Analysis

Multiple recommendations may exist.

Example:

Situation:

Terminal Congestion

Possible Actions:

Option A

Continue Operations

Option B

Slow Operations

Option C

Redirect Cargo

Option D

Suspend Intake

The reasoning engine evaluates all options before selecting one.

---

# 8. Decision Ranking Logic

Every candidate decision is ranked using:

* Safety Impact
* Operational Impact
* Economic Impact
* Environmental Impact
* Infrastructure Impact
* Regulatory Impact

Highest overall value receives highest priority.

---

# 9. Confidence Propagation

Confidence is inherited through reasoning layers.

Observation Confidence
↓
Signal Confidence
↓
Pattern Confidence
↓
Context Confidence
↓
Decision Confidence

Weak evidence reduces confidence.

Strong evidence increases confidence.

---

# 10. Causal Reasoning

Every recommendation must answer:

What happened?

Why?

What caused it?

What contributed?

What happens next?

What alternatives exist?

What if conditions change?

Example:

Water Level Drop
↓
Reduced Navigability
↓
Cargo Risk
↓
Draft Restriction
↓
Recommendation

Every link must be explainable.

---

# 11. Conflict Resolution

Conflicts occur when recommendations disagree.

Example:

Opportunity Engine:

Increase Cargo Movement

Risk Engine:

Reduce Navigation Activity

Conflict Resolution Process:

Risk Assessment
↓
Safety Evaluation
↓
Policy Evaluation
↓
Priority Ranking
↓
Final Recommendation

# 15. Success Criteria

The Marine Reasoning Engine is successful when:

* Every recommendation is explainable.
* Every decision has evidence.
* Every risk has traceability.
* Every opportunity has justification.
* Every recommendation can be replayed.
* Every confidence score can be defended.
* Every operator can understand why the system reached a conclusion.

The engine should behave like an experienced Commodore who combines observation, memory, evidence and judgment into a single explainable operational decision.
